#Week4
**Overview of Accelerated ATAM Process:**

- The Accelerated ATAM (Architecture Tradeoff Analysis Method) simplifies the traditional ATAM by eliminating the formal presentation step.
- Steps 2-6 are emphasized, with steps 7 and 8 combined into steps 5 and 6.
- The results are presented quickly in a short meeting scenario.

**Steps 2-6 Overview:**

1. **Business Goals & Drivers:**
    
    - The business goals of the Arizona Investment Bank (AIB) system include improving customer satisfaction, expanding the customer base, and retaining customers.
    - Achieved through providing reliable, fast, and accessible bank services via web applications and future mobile applications.
    - **Quality Attributes:**
        - System must ensure 24/7 availability with recovery within 30 minutes of failure.
        - High priority on data confidentiality and integrity.
        - Future functionality expansion, including mobile devices and new account types.
2. **Key Functionality:**
    
    - The AIB system serves two main user groups: customers and bank representatives.
    - Bank representatives handle account types (checking, savings) and customer transactions (deposit, withdrawal, transfers).
    - **Future Expansion:**
        - Plans to add a mobile app for users to perform the same functions.
3. **Architecture Presentation:**
    
    - **Current Architecture:**
        - **Client-Server Model:** Web application (HTML/JS) communicates via HTTP with the bank’s Java-based server (J2EE).
        - The server handles banking functionality and communicates with a relational database (Oracle) using JDBC.
    - **Technology Realization:**
        - The system uses Java servlets, JavaBeans, and JSPs on the server side, with client applications interacting via the web browser.
4. **Architectural Approaches:**
    
    - **Availability:**
        - The architecture includes a synchronized backup server for 24/7 availability.
    - **Security:**
        - Customers authenticate via login/password with a 0.01% chance of unauthorized access.
        - Data is encrypted to ensure security with a breach probability under 0.01%.
    - **Modifiability:**
        - The system should accommodate various client applications, including mobile apps.
        - Modifications for new account types (e.g., investment accounts) are a high business priority but technically easier to implement.
5. **Utility Tree:**
    
    - **High Priority Quality Attributes:**
        - **Availability** and **Security** are key, followed by **Modifiability**.
        - Attributes are categorized based on business priority and technical difficulty (e.g., recoverability is high in priority but difficult to implement).
6. **Evaluation of Current Architecture:**
    
    - While the current client-server architecture supports availability and security, **modifiability** is challenging due to tightly coupled client and server technologies.
    - Adding new client types (mobile, etc.) will be difficult because of this tight coupling.

**Proposed Solution (Service-Oriented Architecture):**

- **Enterprise Service Bus (ESB):**
    - Introduces a layer between the client and server to decouple technologies and provide a uniform interface to clients.
    - This approach allows different client types (e.g., web, mobile) to interact with the server independently of the server's technology.
    - **Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA)** with web APIs (REST) will allow easy expansion to new client types.

**Comparison of Architectures:**

- **Client-Server vs. SOA:**
    - The client-server model is limited by the need for compatibility between client and server technologies, restricting new client integrations.
    - The SOA model, with its ESB, facilitates easy integration of new clients by using platform- and language-independent APIs.

**Final Evaluation & Presentation:**

- **Reconciliation of Architectural Approaches:**
    - Various architectural solutions are compared to assess risk, feasibility, and alignment with business goals.
    - A final recommendation is made based on the evaluation of each architecture's potential to meet key quality attributes.

**Documentation and Presentation:**

- In an accelerated ATAM, the final documentation is presented as a technical presentation with basic architecture documentation for stakeholders.

**Key Takeaways:**

- **Key Goals of the AIB System:**
    
    - Improve customer satisfaction, expand the customer base, and retain customers through reliable and accessible bank services.
- **Quality Attributes to Focus On:**
    
    - Availability (24/7 uptime, recovery within 30 minutes)
    - Security (data confidentiality and integrity)
    - Modifiability (support for future client types, e.g., mobile apps)
- **Architectural Evaluation:**
    
    - The current architecture meets some goals but faces challenges in **modifiability**.
    - **Service-Oriented Architecture (SOA)** with an **Enterprise Service Bus (ESB)** is a proposed solution for future scalability and flexibility.
- **Utility Tree & Evaluation:**
    
    - Identify high-priority quality attributes, such as **availability**, **security**, and **modifiability**, and assess current architecture's ability to meet them.
- **Final Recommendation:**
    
    - Consider adopting **SOA** with **web APIs** for improved **modifiability** and ease of integration for future client types, overcoming limitations of the current client-server model.
