#### Definition and Fundamentals

1. **Autonomous Systems**:
    
    - Perform specific, automated tasks without human intervention.
    - Adapt dynamically to changes in their environment, meaning tasks are not strictly predefined.
    - Capable of **self-correction** and **self-learning** from previous experiences.
2. **Characteristics of Autonomous Systems**:
    
    - Must perform complex, adaptive tasks autonomously.
    - Examples include self-driving cars, social robots, and military applications.
    - Require **intelligence** to adapt and learn, allowing for minimal human intervention.

#### Intelligent Systems

1. **DARPA's Definition of Intelligent Systems**:
    - Should be able to **infer and reason** from a knowledge base.
    - Able to **learn from experiences** and improve over time.
    - Must be **self-aware** and capable of reflecting on and correcting their behavior.
    - Need to **respond robustly to unexpected scenarios** and interact naturally with humans when necessary.
    - Important in applications that require **human interaction**, like social robots and medical robots in ICUs or surgeries.

#### Application Areas of Autonomous Systems

1. **Automotive**:
    
    - Autonomous vehicles (e.g., self-driving cars) expected to reach 15% market share by 2030.
    - Equipped with **Advanced Driver Assistance Systems (ADAS)** like lane detection, automatic braking.
    - **Key companies**: General Motors, Waymo, Ford, Volkswagen, Toyota’s e-Palette (Mobility-as-a-Service).
    - Requires real-time data processing, electronics, and mechanical systems for independent functioning.
2. **Healthcare**:
    
    - Used in surgeries (e.g., da Vinci surgical robots) and patient care.
    - Types: surgical robots, rehabilitation robots (e.g., exoskeletons), pharmacy robots.
    - Demands high accuracy and **real-time data gathering and processing** within medical devices.
3. **Manufacturing**:
    
    - Utilized in **supply chain management, assembly lines, and quality control**.
    - Includes workplace robots automating repetitive or dangerous tasks.
4. **IT Industry**:
    
    - Autonomous systems manage data warehouses, **cloud-based systems, and fault tolerance**.
    - Autonomous handling of resource allocation and server maintenance improves efficiency and customer service.
5. **Military**:
    
    - Extensively used for **intelligence gathering, surveillance**, and handling tasks too dangerous for humans.
    - Significant in improving safety and operational capability in the field.

#### Computational Requirements

1. **Real-time Data Acquisition and Processing**:
    
    - Needs to acquire and interpret various data types (e.g., text, video, sensor data) in real-time.
    - Must make **appropriate, intelligent decisions** quickly and autonomously, emulating human responses to certain scenarios.
2. **Actuators for Physical Responses**:
    
    - Actions are executed by **actuators** (robotic hands, legs, etc.) that perform physical tasks based on decisions.
    - These systems must respond precisely and at the right time to carry out programmed tasks.
3. **Multidisciplinary Knowledge for Development**:
    
    - Developing autonomous systems involves **math, physics, mechanical engineering, computer science, AI, and machine learning**.
    - Requires knowledge from diverse areas for successful design and implementation, making it a challenging and rewarding field.

#### The Future of Robotics and Autonomous Systems

1. **Impact on Society**:
    
    - Expected to be transformative, similar to telecommunication and the Internet.
    - Increasingly integrated into critical industries, including healthcare, automotive, and military sectors.
2. **Technical Summary**:
    
    - Key features include **real-time data acquisition, data processing, and actuator movement** to execute tasks autonomously.
    - Requires collaboration across disciplines to create robust, intelligent, and self-sufficient systems.
