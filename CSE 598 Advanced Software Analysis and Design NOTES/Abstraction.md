- Refers to the process of simplifying complex systems by focusing on the essential features and ignoring unnecessary details. It allows us to manage complexity by dealing with a simplified representation of an object, concept, or system, which highlights what is important for a particular context.

### **In Object-Oriented Programming (OOP):**

- **Abstract Classes**: A blueprint for other classes. It cannot be instantiated directly but provides a base for subclasses to extend or implement its methods.
- **Abstraction in OOP**: Hiding complex implementation details and exposing only the relevant functionalities to the user. For example, when you use a car, you don't need to know the inner workings of the engine—you only need to know how to drive it (start, stop, steer).
